CSCI339001 Visualization

Teaser Image (TBD)

Data can capture a snapshot of the world and allow us to understand ourselves and our communities better. With ever-increasing amounts of data deeply immersed in our lives, the ability to understand and communicate data is now becoming essential for everyone. Visualization leverages our visual perception to provide a powerful yet accessible way to make sense of large and complex data. It has been widely adopted across different disciplines, from science and engineering to business and journalism, to combat the overabundance of information in our society. In this course, students will learn foundational knowledge about how to design effective visualizations for analysis and presentation based on theories and principles from graphic design, perceptual psychology, and cognitive science. Students will also learn practical skills about how to rapidly explore and communicate data using Tableau and build interactive visualization products (e.g., articles, tools, and systems) using web-based frameworks including D3.js.

Course Policy

No laptop policy
Students may not use laptops, tablets, phones or other electronic devices during the lectures except when explicitly asked to do so. There is plenty of evidence that multitasking incurs a high cognitive cost in memory and attention (ref), students who take notes on laptops may learn less than those who take notes on paper (ref), laptops adversely impact not only the people using them, but also those around them (ref).

Attendance
A lot of learning in this course happens through various activities in both lectures and labs. Not only attending but also being on time is crucial.

Late submission
For individual assignments, we give a penalty of 10% reduction in grade for each late day. For the final project, the same 10% penalty applies to the written report, but students must present their work in the class presentation day.
